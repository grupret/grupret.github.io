Pushkar, Rajasthan

December 2023

The Sacred City of Brahma

Pushkar is one of those rare places where spirituality and vibrant culture merge seamlessly. The holy lake, surrounded by 52 ghats, creates an atmosphere of tranquility that's hard to find elsewhere. Walking through the narrow lanes filled with colorful shops selling everything from camel leather goods to silver jewelry, you can feel the pulse of ancient traditions still very much alive.

The highlight of my visit was witnessing the evening aarti at the ghats. The synchronized chanting, the flickering diyas reflecting on the lake's surface, and the energy of hundreds of devotees created a moment of pure magic. It reminded me why travel isn't just about seeing new placesâ€”it's about experiencing the soul of a destination.

Key Experiences:
  • Morning meditation by the sacred lake
  • Exploring the only Brahma temple in the world
  • Sunset camel safari in the Thar Desert
  • Shopping in the vibrant bazaars

Shimla & Kufri, Himachal Pradesh

March 2023

Colonial Charm Meets Mountain Serenity

Shimla, the former summer capital of British India, carries the elegant charm of colonial architecture nestled in the lap of the Himalayas. The famous Mall Road, with its Victorian-era buildings and bustling markets, tells stories of a bygone era while serving as the heart of modern-day Shimla.

The short drive to Kufri opened up a completely different world. At 2,720 meters above sea level, Kufri offered panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan ranges. The crisp mountain air and the silence broken only by occasional bird calls provided the perfect environment for reflectionâ€” something every software architect needs after months of debugging complex systems!

Memorable Moments:
  • Toy train ride from Kalka to Shimla
  • Exploring the Viceregal Lodge
  • Horse riding in Kufri
  • Sampling local Himachali cuisine

Hampi, Karnataka

January 2023

A Journey Through Time

Hampi is like stepping into a open-air museum where every stone has a story to tell. The ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, scattered across a landscape of giant boulders and palm groves, create one of the most surreal travel experiences I've ever had. As someone who works with complex architectural systems in the digital world, I was humbled by the sophistication of these 14th-century architectural marvels.

The Virupaksha Temple, still functioning after 700 years, stands as a testament to the engineering brilliance of its creators. Climbing Matanga Hill for sunrise revealed the full scope of this ancient cityâ€”a reminder that great architecture, whether in stone or code, withstands the test of time.

Ancient Wonders Explored:
  • Sunrise from Matanga Hill
  • Virupaksha Temple architecture study
  • Coracle ride on Tungabhadra River
  • Lotus Mahal and Elephant Stables

Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu

August 2022

The Princess of Hill Stations

Kodaikanal, wrapped in mist and dotted with shola forests, provided the perfect escape from the digital world. The star-shaped Kodai Lake, man-made but beautifully integrated with nature, became my daily walking meditation spot. There's something profoundly calming about morning boat rides surrounded by eucalyptus trees and the constant companionship of cool mountain air.

The trek to Pillar Rocks and the walks along Coaker's Walk offered spectacular valley views that reminded me why we need to occasionally disconnect from our screens and reconnect with the natural world. The experience of coding on my laptop while overlooking misty valleys was surprisingly productiveâ€”nature has a way of clearing mental blocks!

Misty Mountain Memories:
  • Early morning boat rides on Kodai Lake
  • Trekking to Pillar Rocks viewpoint
  • Exploring Shola forests and waterfalls
  • Photography walks in Bryant Park
